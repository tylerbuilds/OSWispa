Model guide

Download the right model for your hardware.

Bigger Whisper models sound better, but they also slow down faster and can make a modest machine feel heavy. Use this guide to understand what OSWispa will usually auto-pick and what that means in practice.

Rule of thumb

CPU-only machines should expect `base.en` or `small.en`. Apple Silicon and discrete GPUs can step up to `medium.en` or `distil-large-v3` when there is real headroom.

OSWispa tries to protect responsiveness first. `large-v3` is manual, not the normal default.

CPU-only defaults

base.en / small.en

Intel Macs and older laptops.

Apple Silicon defaults

small / medium / distil

Big GPU defaults

medium / distil

Chosen with safe headroom.

Guidance

What to expect on real hardware.

These are practical starting points, not benchmark theater. OSWispa biases toward staying responsive.

base.en

The usual default for CPU-only machines.

  • Download size: 142 MB
  • Typical pick: Intel Macs and older laptops
  • What to expect: quick output, lighter system impact, more misses on names and noisy audio
Download base.en

small.en

A step up when the machine can take it.

  • Download size: 466 MB
  • Typical pick: stronger CPU-only desktops, lighter Apple Silicon Macs
  • What to expect: better wording than base.en, but still not “instant” on long recordings
Download small.en

medium.en

A realistic step up when memory and acceleration are available.

  • Download size: 1.5 GB
  • Typical pick: Apple Silicon with headroom, mid-range GPUs
  • What to expect: better recovery of phrasing and punctuation, but more delay on CPU-only systems
Download medium.en

distil-large-v3 / large-v3

Only for machines with real headroom or people who care more about accuracy than latency.

  • `distil-large-v3`: best English speed/accuracy on capable hardware
  • `large-v3`: highest accuracy, but the heaviest and slowest option
  • What to expect: great results when the machine is strong, frustrating lag when it is not
Download distil-large-v3 Download large-v3

Automatic model picks

  • Older Intel Macs and other CPU-only machines usually land on `base.en`.
  • Faster CPU-only desktops can move up to `small.en`.
  • Apple Silicon 8-16 GB systems usually land on `small.en` or `medium.en`.
  • Apple Silicon with large unified memory or discrete GPUs with healthy VRAM can reach `distil-large-v3`.
  • `large-v3` is not the normal default because OSWispa treats responsiveness as the safer first choice.
